The Language Immersion Student Residence

One of BYU's hidden secrets is that of the Language Immersion Student Residence (LISR). The LISR is an apartment complex where each apartment is dedicated to a specific lanugage. The students who apply to live there sign up for a specific language and promise to speak only that language while in the apartment.

And the LISR isn't just a place for these students to live. Living at the LISR is considered a BYU 1-2 credit class. Students are required to pay a small program fee to live there. With that fee residents of the LISR are required weekly to grocery shop, prepare dinners, and eat said dinners with their roommates. In doing this these students are not only getting more time to practice their assigned language, but also are given the opportunity to grow close with their roommates while eating a healthier diet.
